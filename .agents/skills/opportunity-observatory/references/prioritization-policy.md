# Inclusion and selection policy

Do not calculate a weighted score.

## Include qualified findings

Include a report or site finding when every condition is satisfied:

- Direct, traceable evidence supports the central claim.
- It is relevant to a named audience or a concrete operational need.
- Medicaid Audit Intelligence can contribute something more useful than a generic summary.
- It is new, changed, newly relevant, or materially useful to reconsider.
- No rejection condition applies.

Surface every qualified finding discovered within the run's disclosed source window and site-review scope. There is no row quota or arbitrary minimum or maximum. Deduplicate related evidence into one row when it supports the same action, and keep rows concise enough for one maintainer to scan.

## Select the best next move

Recommend zero or one next move. Select it only when:

- the evidence is strong enough to act on;
- the expected reader or operational benefit is concrete;
- one maintainer can complete and review a bounded change;
- it is more useful now than the other qualified findings; and
- important risks and verification steps can be stated plainly.

The recommendation may be report ingestion, an existing-page improvement, cross-report synthesis, navigation or internal linking, a search-intent correction, technical maintenance, or another bounded product/content improvement. If none qualifies, say so. Do not force the report inventory into an action ranking.

## Reject or defer

Do not include a candidate when it:

- cannot be verified from a primary source;
- duplicates or cannibalizes an existing page without a consolidation plan;
- depends mainly on inferred search demand;
- requires legal or clinical interpretation outside the site's role;
- relies on an unstable or ambiguous source;
- is mainly cosmetic or metadata-only without measured need;
- would create more ongoing operational work than its likely value supports.

## Solo-maintainer constraint

Make the output easy to scan in roughly ten minutes even when the inventory is long: use tables, concise cells, and deduplication. Recommend no more than one new implementation at a time. Prefer improving an existing page or ingesting a high-value report over creating a new content program.
